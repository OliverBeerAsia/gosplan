# Art Direction Standard

## Visual Promise

GOSPLAN should look like a Soviet-era city planning board that became a living city:
- strict geometry
- utilitarian materials
- strong red/gold propaganda accents
- readable simulation overlays at all zoom levels

## Building Language

- Residential:
  - Khrushchyovka and panel blocks dominate.
  - Repeatable prefab forms with facade variance, weathering, and utility attachments.
- Industrial:
  - heavy silhouettes, stacks, gantries, loading yards, soot and pipe details.
- Civic:
  - clear public identity: schools, clinics, metro entries, party and cultural buildings.
- Landmarks:
  - monument/plaza assets should be sparse and compositionally strong.

## Soviet Queue Motif

Queues are part of city storytelling and should visually reflect scarcity and pressure.
- Queue props appear around government/service buildings.
- Queue length scales with service stress and demand.
- Queue color language stays grounded: coats, caps, muted uniforms, occasional red accents.

## Terrain and District Grounding

- Terrain should never read as flat wallpaper.
- Ground materials must include variation, seams, and lived-in marks (stains, dirt specks, wear).
- District context should emerge through combined terrain, props, and building variants.

## Overlay Readability Rules

- Zoning overlays use planning-map patterns, not just solid color blocks.
- Coverage overlays must remain legible over detailed terrain.
- UI and overlays should preserve gameplay readability first, style second.

## Quality Tiers

- Low:
  - preserve gameplay readability and clear silhouettes.
- Medium:
  - include major edge and state visuals without high sprite density.
- High:
  - full terrain detail, richer queue visuals, maximum atmosphere layers.

## Asset Constraints

- Keep tile readability at distance.
- Preserve camera-agnostic contrast and silhouette.
- Avoid style drift away from Soviet brutalist/poster aesthetic.
